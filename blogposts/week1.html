Week 1 Reflection:

Monday, March 3, 2025 & Wednesday March 5, 2025

Monday of Week 1 was our introduction to video and video qualities class, as well as a camera and tripod class on Wednesday, where we learnt about tripod handling techniques and features of the Sony PXW-X70 camera. During Monday's class, I worked on an in-class group activity. As a group we were assigned to the Voice/Witness quality of video. Go to We spent around 20 minutes reading through the Voice/Witness module and browsing the examples. After, my group brainstormed some examples of voice/witness that we have noticed in video or seen on tv, movies, art, ads, docs etc. As a group we made a list of all our examples, which included:

  • Vlogs.
  • Social Media.
  • Daily Uni Life, Day in the life videos.
  • Interviews, asking questions to strangers.
  • Gabby documentary on Social Media, daily blogger.
  • Situation draws attention because of a regular person.
  • Social Media encourages people to share anything or everything.
  • Encourages people to bring awareness to events or suspicions about certain topics.

    • The task for Week 1 was a Video Diary, in which we had to share and take note of a video we have been watching recently. In Wednesday's practical, we discussed it as a class, each sharing our video. I hadn't been watching much lately, though I did watch a few clips of David Attenborough's Blue Planet. I took note of the documentary and how it is an example of Voice/Witness:

    Attenborough's nature focused documentaries capture the life of nature, witnessing first hand daily struggles and events of wildlife across the globe. Alongside capturing nature, David Attenborough's voice-over enhances the remarkable discoveries, as these commentaries allow viewers to gain comprehensive knowledge on nature which most have not encountered before.

    For our Wednesday practical we delved into the handling techniques of a tripod and guide to using the Sony PXW-X70 camera. It was my first time using this type of camera, an insightful learning experience. A few features of the camera which we covered included, Exposure/Iris, Shutter Speed, Gain, White Balance, Resolution and Mic inputs.
