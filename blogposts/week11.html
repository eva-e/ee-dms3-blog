Week 11 Reflection:

Monday, May 19, 2025 & Wednesday May 21, 2025

On May the 26th and 27th I began editing and composing the soundtrack. I was in a tight situation as I had been struggling to create a narrative order for my footage and decide on a soundtrack. The process that I followed was unusual and disorderly however I will explain it here in my reflection.
It is evident I was extremely behind. From the feedback in week 9, I worked on a mixture of soundtrack composition and watching a few scenes at a time. I started on my piano by composing a simple melody for an intro and title screen. As I was composing the simple melody I went through the videos and chose which clip was suitable for a title screen, able to clearly show the title and introduce the location, similar to an Establishing Shot.
I was happy with the hallway/entrance to a room in the NGV which had a pleasing symmetrical aesthetic, revealing three artworks. While playing the title video on loop countless times, I was playing the simple piano melody. The melody sparked another idea for the next sequence of videos. As this melody was quite repetitive I decided to make a fast montage of different clips, with the melody also increasing in speed to correlate with the speed of the montage. Next I created something similar to a drop, where the music drops from its momentum to a soft ambience of strings and piano melody. I was working with two screens, one laptop recording the sounds on FL Studio and the other showing Premiere Pro with the video timeline and edits. After this sequence I proceeded to choose videos to fill in my timeline. However before proceeding to the next sequence I composed the whole soundtrack. I imported the soundtrack which was around 2 minutes and 30 seconds in length and went by this length to fill in the timeline with videos. Lastly, I repeated the fast montage from the beginning to bring the portraits back into perspective, a reminder to the audience of this obsession to remain youthful, however naturally we are ever changing, hence the rapid nature of this montage and change between portraits hinting at how our appearance changes.
